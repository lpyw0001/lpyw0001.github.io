Concluding Remarks

In summary, this project analyses Mastercard's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The main goals analysed include zero hunger, gender equality, decent work and economic growth, sustainable cities and communities, partnerships and climate change, and through this analysis we can see that Mastercard is closely linked to the achievement of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), contributing to the eradication of poverty, the promotion of economic and social development and the protection of the environment, and that the measures taken by the company are effective and worthy of replication by other companies working together to achieve the UN SDGs.

In further analysis, it was found that Mastercard needs to take steps to mitigate climate change and ensure not only climate-related initiatives but also a broader development strategy for long-term success. Mastercard's sustainability commitment includes reducing its carbon footprint in its business and giving consumers the opportunity to reduce their carbon footprint, for example by replacing plastic cards with environmentally friendly materials. Environmental issues are very important in today's society as they can be very damaging to many sectors, with climate deterioration endangering food security, human health, economic development, etc. For this reason we propose the use of economic incentives to encourage investors to protect the environment, as the economic effect is the most important for investors. As well as putting pressure on governments to use financial instruments to invest in carbon capture or technological improvements, etc., only if governments take effective measures can society as a whole respond in a positive and orderly manner.
